⬡ The Seven Realms ⬡

Explore the Zones

Seven immersive realms carved from 125 acres of real Peoria terrain — each a chapter in the Dragon's legend.

I
The Gateway

Miner's Welcome Station

Real terrain: Parking lot & trailhead at 525 N Kickapoo Creek Rd

Enter the Dragon's Forge through an imposing Coal Creek-style heritage entrance gate. The Miner's Welcome Station — styled as a 19th-century mine office — greets adventurers with their quest scroll. Dragon scale pavement patterns in the cobblestones lead the way from the parking area to the trail's mouth.

Parking & Payment

Rocky Glen's small paved lot at 525 N Kickapoo Creek Rd fills quickly on weekends. The Dragon's Forge concept expands parking with curated overflow lots on nearby private land, all payable through modern contactless systems.

II
The Ascent

The Dragon's Spine

Real terrain: Steep 140ft vertical climb with rope handholds and stairs

The most thrilling section of the trail becomes a legendary ascent up the sleeping dragon's back. Rocky Glen's existing 140-foot vertical climb — complete with rope handholds and carved stone stairs — is reimagined as scaling the Dragon's Spine. Industrial mine cart rails line the switchbacks. Steampunk cog-wheel markers count each elevation gain.

Structures & Features

  • Mine cart rail system decorating the 140ft switchback climb
  • Coal lantern posts marking each level of the ascent
  • Cog-wheel & gear decorative elements (steampunk industrial)
  • "Dragon's Fin" carved stone handholds on existing rope-climb route
  • Elevation markers: "100 feet above the dragon's heart"
III
The Glen

Dragon's Tears Falls

Real terrain: Sandstone waterfall & box canyon — the "Miniature Starved Rock"

Rocky Glen's centerpiece — the sandstone waterfall known as "Peoria's Miniature Starved Rock" — becomes Dragon's Tears Falls, where legend says the last great dragon wept as the miners took the coal. The box canyon's jagged sandstone walls are "Dragon Teeth." Orange-painted stepping stones cross the Molten River. A Coal Creek-style blacksmith shop, "The Forge," stands watch at the canyon's edge.

Structures & Features

  • "The Forge" — Coal Creek-style blacksmith workshop building
  • Interactive water play area in the creek bed
  • "Molten River" stepping stones (orange-glazed ceramic)
  • "Dragon Teeth" sandstone formation interpretive plaques
  • Seasonal winter ice display area (falls freeze annually)
IV
The Mine Shaft

The Dragon's Lair

Real terrain: Existing gated coal mine shaft at mile 0.6 on the trail

The most dramatic stop on the trail — Rocky Glen's actual gated coal mine shaft, documented since the 1870s, is reborn as the Dragon's Lair. The arched entrance is decorated as a dragon's maw with stone-carved teeth. Within view of the gate: a Coal Creek-style Mine Museum, interactive equipment displays, and the legendary "Dragon Egg" boulder climbing feature. Geocache quest: "Find the Dragon's Heart."

Structures & Features

  • Coal Creek-style Mine Museum building (1870s–1920s era)
  • Dragon maw arch decoration on existing mine shaft gate
  • Interactive mining equipment (drill press, ore cart, crusher replicas)
  • "Dragon Egg" boulder climbing features (5 oversized eggs)
  • Quest: "Find the Dragon's Heart" (geocache-style adventure)
V
The Bowl

The Dragon's Nest

Real terrain: "The Bowl" — a mysterious hidden area, favorite of hikers

Deep within Rocky Glen's most secluded and beloved hidden zone — "The Bowl" — lies the Dragon's Nest. This fantasy playground is the heart of the concept: an immersive dragon-themed adventure space where the dragon's enormous body forms a climbable slide, its tail wraps a castle tower, and its eggs become stepping stones. A Coal Creek-style schoolhouse anchors the education programs.

Structures & Features

  • Climbable dragon body structure with 20-foot tail slide
  • Castle tower with rope bridges and observation deck
  • Dragon egg stepping stone field (8 oversized eggs)
  • "Academy of Scales" — Coal Creek schoolhouse for STEM programs
  • Sensory garden with dragon-themed native plantings
VI
Horseshoe Bottoms

Dragon's Forge Village

Real terrain: Horseshoe Bottoms — former coal mining area with hundreds of mining traces

The crown jewel of Dragon's Forge — a full Coal Creek-inspired heritage village reconstructed in Horseshoe Bottoms, where English coal miners worked the land from the 1870s. Eight signature buildings anchor a living history experience. The Dragon's Breath Railway — a small-gauge scenic train — circles the village, passing working demonstrations of heritage crafts. "Coal is so abundant in this neighborhood that there is no danger of the supply falling in a thousand years." — C. Balance, 1870.

VII
The Ridge Trail

Dragon's Watch

Real terrain: Top of the Glen spur — ridge trail with panoramic overlooks

Crowning the entire journey — Rocky Glen's ridge trail becomes Dragon's Watch, where the dragon surveys its entire territory from on high. Observation platforms styled as mine tipple structures command panoramic views of the Kickapoo Creek valley below. Industrial telescopes let visitors spy every zone they've conquered. Picnic shelters in heritage mine styling complete the summit experience.

Structures & Features

  • Three observation platforms styled as mine tipple structures
  • Industrial telescopes and viewing stations
  • Picnic shelters in heritage mine architecture styling
  • "Dragon's Territory" panoramic map interpretation boards
  • Quest completion ceremony site — "Dragon Knight" investiture platform

Coal Creek Community Park

Korumburra, Australia's Coal Creek Community Park preserves 53 heritage buildings across 27 hectares — a living 1870s–1920s coal mining village with working steam trains, heritage craft demonstrations, and 22 community groups calling it home.

The Rocky Glen Translation

Rocky Glen's Horseshoe Bottoms — already containing hundreds of visible mining traces and the actual gated mine shaft — provides an authentic canvas. The fantasy layer doesn't replace the history; it unlocks it. Every dragon legend maps to a real coal mining story.

Community Vision

Following Coal Creek's model: 22 community groups, curriculum-based education programs, heritage craft volunteers, flora and fauna sanctuary, and a self-sustaining heritage engine that serves Peoria for generations. All anchored by the dragon narrative that draws families in.
